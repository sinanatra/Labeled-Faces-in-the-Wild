This visualisation arranges the faces from Labeled Faces in the Wild, a dataset for image recognition, sorting them by the most common.

The dataset contains more than 13,000 images, each one of them labeled with the name of the person pictured.
Created in 2007, this project is based on Faces in the wild, a dataset made in 2004 containing 30,281 faces collected from News Photographs, in particularly, Yahoo News. Since these images are taken in the wild rather than under fixed laboratory conditions, they represent a broad range of individuals, pose, expression, illumination conditions and time frames Berg et al., 2004, Whoâ€™s in the Picture? In Advances in Neural Information Processing Systems 17: Proceedings of the 2004 Conference [Vol. 17, p. 137]. MIT Press..
Considering that 2004 saw an increase of fighting in the Iraq War and George W. Bush was a main figure in tv reports, this subsequently led him to be the most portrait face in the dataset. Even though the work made in 2007 only selected one third of the images of the previous one, Bush still appeared 530 times.
